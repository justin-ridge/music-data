Overview

The goal of data categorization by utilizing unsupervised machine learning algorithms is to identify overlooked relationships in our data, equipping us with more knowledge to make data-driven decisions when considering unknown data points. We had thousands of songs to consider, so a KMeans cluster analysis was an excellent first step towards making sense of our data.

We utilized the elbow method to determine the optimal number of clusters to generate, and our data suggested that 5 categories would be an ideal balance.


Conclusions

Categories 1 and 5 show the lowest occurence of popular music - songs that share characteristics with these categories can be considered long shots to achieve streaming popularity.

Category 3 (children's music) is a relatively smaller cluster, but the frequency of popular songs shows that producing children's music is a safe bet for streaming popularity.

Category 4 is the largest category, and also has the highest relative frequency of popular songs. This category is worth exploring on its own in the future - identifying clusters within category 4 itself could help to maximize opportunities for popular streaming investments. Category 2 is smaller but also has a decent frequency of popularity - this group is worth analyzing further as well.
